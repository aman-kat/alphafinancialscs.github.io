Loan Against Property
(these doesn't have restrictions like home loans)

Description

As the name suggests loan against property (LAP), is the loan that you avail by keeping your commercial/residential property as collateral with the bank or a financial institution. The value of your property decides the amount of loan you will be sanctioned, generally banks lend up to 50% to 60% of the value of the property and you would've understood as property is kept as collateral with the bank, so obviously it is a secured loan.

How it works?

LAP works in a similar fashion as home loans, you pledge your property to avail a loan from a bank/NBFC and you repay the laon amount within given time with interest.Though, home loans come with restrictions like you can not use them other than buying a home, on the other hand LAP come with no end usage restriction, borrowers can utilise the funds as their will, you can use them to start a new business or expand an existing one, marriages, child's education, medical expenses, debt consolidation and what not.

The lender takes into consideration many factors such as your income, age, spouseâ€™s income, value of property to decide on your loan amount, usually banks lends upto 60% of the value of property, private sector banks may offer upto 75% of the property's value as a loan. In case you fail to repay the loan, the lender has all right to auction your property and recover the money. LAPs are reasonably convenient to avail and repay due to the relatively low interest rate.

1Eligibility Criteria

Age of Applicant: 18 to 70 years for both salaried and self-employed
Income: 25K/per month minimum Income
Property eligibility: Self occupied/ vacant Residential Property, Commercial Property

1Documentation

for salaried individuals
- Identity proof, address proof and age proof
- Salary slips of last 3 months
- Bank statements
- Latest Form-16 and Income Tax Return details
- Title documents of property

for self-employed individuals
- Identity proof, address proof and age proof
- Financial Statement, including balance sheet, profit and loss account statements
- Bank statements
- Income Tax Return (ITR) details of last 3 years
- Title documents of property
- Business existence proof

Benefits

1) Loan Against Property gets processed within 72 hours of applying
2) Loan Against Property is a secured loans, so the interest rates are comparatively lower.
3) Easy Balance Transferring
4) The loan is available against self-occupied residential property, such as house, apartment, flat etc.
5) It is also provided against rented residential properties
6) It is also available to commercial properties like an office building, malls, shopping complex, shops etc.
7) It can be taken against a plot of land owned by the borrower
8) It comes with flexible tenure and lower interest rate compared to personal loans
9) Interest paid for the Loan Against Property provides tax benefits under Section 37 (1) of the Income Tax Act, 1961
10) If the amount is used for funding a house, the interest paid on the loan provides tax benefits also under Section 24 of the Income Tax Act, 1961, up to an amount of Rs. 2 lakh

So, what's the upshot?

Well, Loan Against Property is good option if you have any spare property and finance requirement (already discussed). As there are no restrictions on using funds, you can use them anywhere you want and as these are secured types they offer low interest rates, longer flexible tenures and we have discussed almost all benefits already.
