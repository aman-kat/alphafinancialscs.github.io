Education Loan
(before pursuing higher studies, read this first)

Description

A education loan is a type of loan which help students pay for college educations and other school-related expenses, such as tuition, books and supplies, and living expenses. Payments are often made after a six-month grace period, after graduation.

How it works?

An education loan offers a moratorium period that reduces the immediate burden of the repayment start after the completion of the course. Generally, the repayment starts when the course is completed. So a student can start paying the loan EMIs a year after the completion of the course or 6 months after getting a job, whichever is earlier. During the course period, the bank charges simple interest rate on the loan.

Education loans are issued for the purpose of attending an academic institution and pursuing an academic degree. Education loans can be obtained from the government or through private-sector lending sources. It covers the basic course fee and other related expenses such as (college) accommodation, exam and other miscellaneous charges.

If you don't make your payment, your loan goes into delinquency status. If you still don't pay, your school, the financial institution that made or owns your loan, your loan guarantor, and the federal government can all take action to recover the money you owe for your student loan debt.

If you decided to make payments earlier, there aren't any pre-payment penalties on education loans. You can make extra payments on your student loans or pay them off in-full without paying any fees or other penalties.

1Eligibility criteria

- The person who wanted to persue, undergraduate/postgradute courses, doctoral courses and PhDs, Technical courses, diploma or professional courses can apply for Education loan.
- You can apply if you're going to government collages, private recognized institutes aided by government, professional institutions, International collages and universities.
- Residential or commercial property or plot, fixed deposits can be used as collateral.

Benefits

- Low interest rates
- Easy Availability of Study Loan
- Tax Deductible under section 80E of IT Act
- Loan financing up to Rs.1 crore available
- Loan repayment tenure up to 15 years

Understanding Income-tax Section 80E of IT Act.
You will get tax deduction on the interest paid on your education loan under Section 80E of the Income Tax Act, 1961. This deduction is allowed only for the individuals paying interest on the loan for himself, spouse or children or for the student to whom you're a legal guardian. Please note that the tax deduction is only applicable on the interest of the EMI and not the principal. The deduction covers all fields of study in India and overseas.

You will require a certificate from your bank segregating the principle and interest portions of your EMIs to claim this benefit. You can claim this benefit for 8 years from the year you started repayment or until the interest portion of your loan has been repaid, whichever occurs first. You can deduct the entire interest amount paid from your taxable income.

So, what's the upshot?

If you're pursuing your education to the higher levels and don't want to be burden on anyone, then you should always consider taking education loans. So when you get you education, and get a job at some big company, you can pay the loan amount back and no harms done. In short, education loans are good idea because the price for higher educations are really high.
