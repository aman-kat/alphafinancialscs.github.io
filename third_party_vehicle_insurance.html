Third-party Vehicle Insurance
(there is nothing much to know)

Description

Third-party vehicle insurance offers coverage against claims of damages and losses incurred by a driver who is not covered under the insurance policy means not insured. The policy does not provide any benefit to the insured. However, it covers the insured's legal liability for death/disability of third-party loss or damage to third-party property.

How it works?

As per India rules, under the provisions of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, it is essentially to have a third-party insurance. Third-party insurance is a form of liability insurance purchased by an insured (first-party) from an insurer (second party) for protection against the claims of another (third party). Third-party Insurance comes in handy when your vehicle hits another vehicle, you can't measure the level of damage caused by an accident - it might lead to death as well.

So if the victim register a case claiming for compensation then third party insurance comes into picture. It cover any liability claim arises out of bodily injury, property damage, or death of the third-party. Since the third-party insurance cover is mandatory, all non-life insurance companies have an obligation to provide this cover. In India, automobile dealers arrange for a comprehensive insurance cover along with vehicle registration, this comprehensive cover is an add-on to the mandatory third-party cover.

Types

- Third party Car insurance
- Third party Two-wheeler insurance

Disadvantages

- You will not gonna get any claim for your injuries.
- If your vehicle got stolen you will not get any claim.
- You will not going to receive any claim for your vehicle damages, say if your car's windscreen got damaged, no claim.

Benefits

1) Covers legal liabilities
2) Covers financials losses or property damage
3) Medical expenses in case an injury or even death of third-party comes under third-party cover.
