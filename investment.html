Hello there, Investors!!

Description

Investment can be described in many ways, first lets put it in a simpler manner: an act of putting money, effort, time, etc. into something to make a profit. The term investment can refer to any mechanism used for generating future income.

Now lets describe it further in two ways, Economic and Finance.
An investment in an ecomomic, is the purchase of goods that are not consumed today but are used in the future to create wealth (in economic, investments means longer-term savings).
An investment in finance, is a purchase of assets (shares of a company), so that in future the assets will provide the income or you can sell them later at a higher price for a profit.

How it works?

Investing is putting money to work to start or expand projects - or to purchase assets - where those funds are then put to work, with the goal to income and increased value over time. Every investor invests with a specific objective in mind, and each investment has its own unique set of benefits and risks. Investors think a lot about, how big a company's valuation can grow to over time, because they need to get there money back and make huge profits.

Investors can be repaid in several ways, they can be repaid on a “straight schedule” (if they are providing loans instead of buying equity), based upon their percentage of ownership, or they can be paid back at a “preferred rate” of return. Safety is the main concern in investment, some investors do not take much risk and some are more risk tolerant, thus more willing to lose some of their principal in return for the chance of generating a higher profit.

Investments can be stocks, bonds, mutual funds, interest-bearing accounts, land, derivatives, real estate, artwork, old comic books, jewelry etc.

Advantages

- You Stay Ahead of Inflation
- Investing Will Help You Build Wealth
- Investing Will Get You to Retirement (Or Early Retirement)
- Investing Can Help You Save on Taxes

Disadvantages

- Risk losing it all
- Takes time to research
- Emotional ups and downs
- Stockholders are paid last
- Professional competition

Investment Banks

Investment banking is a specific division of banking related to the creation of capital for other companies, governments and other entities. The institution mainly focuses on investment such as trading and asset management, this does not include traditional consumer banking. Investment banks also provide guidance with an IPO or rights offering and help to facilitate mergers and acquisitions, reorganizations.

The advisory division of an investment bank (IB) is paid a fee for their services, while the trading division experiences profit or loss based on its market performance. Professionals who work for investment banks may have careers as financial advisors, traders or salespeople. An investment banking career can be very lucrative, but it typically comes with long hours and significant stress.

Major investment banks: JPMorgan Chase, Goldman Sachs, Bank of America , Morgan Stanley, Citigroup, Barclays Capital, Credit Suisse, Deutsche Bank, Wells Fargo and UBS.

Venture Capitals (VCs)

Venture capital is a type of private equity that investors provide to startup companies and small businesses that are believed to have long-term growth potential. The returns to the venture capitalists depend upon the growth of the company. Venture capitalists have the power to influence major decisions of the companies.

Major Venture Capital firms (in India): Accel Partners, Sequoia Capital, Nexus venture partners, Kalaari Capital, Blume Ventures and Matrix Partners.

Angel Investors

An angel investor is a person who invests in small business ventures, providing capital for start-up or expansion in exchange they ask for ownership equity or convertible debt. Angel investors are also known as informal investors, angel funders, private investors, seed investors or business angels.

Difference between VCs and Angel Investors

Angel investors are wealthy individuals who invest their own money into companies, on the other hand, Venture capitalists (VCs) are employees of venture capital firms that invest other people's money (which they hold in a fund) into companies.
