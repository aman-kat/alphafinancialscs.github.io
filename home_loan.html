Home Loans
(it is a good choice)

Description

A home/housing loan, also known as a mortgage, is an amount of money borrowed by an individual (you), usually from banks and NBFCs to buy a house, simple. A home loan is a secured loan as your property/home (basically just papers) is used by the lender as a colletral, if you default on the loan, the lender holds the right to take possession of your home and sell it to pay off your debts.

How it works?

It works as other secured loans works, you pledge your property as a colletral to the financial institutions in exchange they debt the borrower money. Which the borrower has to pay back with interest in EMI's over a period of time that can vary between 10-30 years depending on your lender and the loan agreement. Financial institutions usually provide up to 80-85% of the market value of the house as loan amount. The bank or financial institution will hold the title or deed to the property till the loan has been paid back wih interest.

Deed of Trust – this document lists the legal obligations and rights of you and the lender. It also states the lender's right to foreclose on the home if you default on the loan. However, there are also certain tax benefits available on home loan under the Section 80EE of Income tax act. The Income tax deduction can be claimed on home loan interest by first time home buyers only. The interest rate on these loans is either fixed, floating or hybrid based on your agreement.

1Eligibilty Criteria

- Applicant must be 18 years old but not more than 70 years
- Income required: 25K per month (minimum) or above
- Work Experienced 3 years or above (for salaried), and there must be 5 years of business stability (for self-employed)
- Minimum Cibil required is Above 650
- Up to 3 earning family members may be added to get higher loan eligibility

Benefits

- As a secured loan it comes with low interest rates

- Flexible tenure, from 10 years to 30 years

- Easy balance transfer, means the outstanding amount with one lender can be transferred to another lender, which offers better loan terms.

- Tax Benefit on Interest paid: As per Section 24(b) of the Income Tax Act, 1961 a deduction up to Rs. 1.5 lakh towards the total interest payable on the home loan towards purchase / construction of house property can be claimed while computing the income from house property. (The deduction stands reduced to Rs. 30,000 in case of loans taken prior to March 01, 1999).

The interest payable for the pre-acquisition or pre-construction period would be deductible in five equal annual instalments commencing from the year in which the house has been acquired or constructed.

- Tax Benefit on Principal Repayment: As per the newly introduced Sections 80C read with section 80CCE of the Income Tax Act, 1961 the principal repayment up to Rs. 1 lakh on your home loan will be allowed as a deduction from the gross total income subject to fulfilment of prescribed conditions.

- Additional Tax Deduction on Home Loan: Home loan borrowers get additional tax deduction of up to Rs. 50,000 and Rs. 1.5 lakh under Section 80EE and Section 80EEA, respectively. To claim the tax benefit under Section 80EE, the home loan amount should be Rs. 35 lakh or less, the value of the property should not exceed Rs. 50 lakh and borrowers should not own any other house on the date of the sanction of loan. To claim tax benefit under Section 80EEA, the stamp value of the property should not exceed Rs. 45 lakh and the borrower should not own any other house on the date of sanction of loan.

Can we repay early?

Yes, of course you can repay a home loan ealry if you want. As a matter of fact if you pay earlier, you will save a lot of money. In case of pre-payment, generally you pay off the principal amount, so your tenure gets reduced and you end up paying less interest. You can only pre-pay your loan amount after paying your first EMI.

So, what's the upshot?

If you're looking to construct or buy a home, you should consider taking home loans beacause they comes with loads of benefits. And as we look ahead in the future the price of properties are raising sky high, you can not just buy a house with your savings. So home loans are important for everybody or whoever wants to buy the house of their dreams.
