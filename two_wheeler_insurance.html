Two-Wheeler Insurance
(it is mandatory by law)

Description

A two wheeler insurance means an agreement between an insurance company and an individual. Where the insurer offers protection against damages incurred in case of an accident, natural disasters, theft and third-party cover also, depends on the type of policy you buy. Two-wheeler insurance is mandatory in India.

How it works?

Riding a bike without a valid two-wheeler insurance plan is considered illegal, so you gotta have one. It works almost as same as a car insurance, but in this case you're driving a two-wheeler. This insurance covers the owner (you) and the two-wheeler (your bike) and it also covers against property damages, death or bodily injury to a third party.

Types

Liability Only Insurance Policy: This policy is mandatory by Indian law, also known as Act Only Policy. It covers losses, property damage, bodily injury or death of a third party and it also covers the owner of the two-wheeler.

Comprehensive Insurance Policy: This covers everything including Liability Only Policy along with additional coverage for damage or theft of the two-wheeler.

Own Damage Policy: This covers the damages to a two-wheeler due to, natural calamities such as fire, flood, lightning, earthquakes, etc. also burglary, theft, riot, strike, malicious acts, terrorist activity, and damage during travel by air, rail, road, water.

The premium depends on various factors

- The engine capacity (CC of your bike)
- The make and model of the bike (e.g make: 2019 & model: XYZ)
- City of registration (e.g Delhi, Mumbai, Gurugram etc)
- Cover you're buying (Third Party policy or Comprehensive Policy)

Benefits

1) If your vehicle gets damaged, your insurance company will take care of fixing it.
2) If the insured suffer total permanent disability, the insurance company will cover you for up to Rs. 1 lakh.
3) And if the insured untimely demise, your family will receive the sum assured from the insurance company.
4) If your vehicle gets stolen, a pre-determined sum assured the Insured Declared Value (IDV) will be provided to you.
5) You also get benefits like No Claim Bonus (NCB).
6) It cover natural disasters and man-made calamities also.
