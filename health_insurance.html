Health Insurance
(protect your family and your savings from severe illnesses)

Description

Lets put it in a simple way, health insurance is an insurance product which covers medical and surgical expenses of an insured individual. It usually pays for losses from accident, medical expense, disability, or sometimes dental expenses and accidental death. The way it typically works is that the consumer (you) pays an up front premium to a health insurance company and in any case there is medical emergancy which leads to hospitalization of the insured, the health insurance company pays your medical bills.

How it works?

Health insurance is an agreement between a health insurance company and you, whereby insurance company agrees to undertake a guarantee of compensation for medical expenses in case the insured falls ill or meets with an accident which leads to hospitalization of the insured. Health insurance helps pay for your health care, it help cover services ranging from routine doctor visits to major medical costs from a serious illness or injury and sometimes it also covers many preventive services to keep you healthy.

Buying a health insurance policy for yourself and your family is important because medical care is expensive and it's one the rise year after year, especially in the private sector. Hospitalisation can burn your all savings and completely disturb your budget. It will become even tough, if the person who brings in the money, is now in a hospital bed. Well you know what to do, you can avoid all this by just paying a small annual premium which would lessen your stress in case of medical emergencies.

Basically, all insurance companies have tie-ups with the leading hospitals so they can provide cashless treatment to the insured. If the insurance company has no tie-ups with the hospital, they reimburse the cost of expenses incurred by the insured. The government also promotes health insurance by providing a deduction from income tax (discussed later in this article).

Types

Individual Health Insurance Plan: This policy covers the health expenses and hospitalization expenses of individual who has taken the policy.

Family Health Insurance Plan: Under this policy, an individual can include all the family members against multiple diseases ubrnder a single cover. These type of plans offers a fixed sum assured for the family members, which can be availed by all members of the family or by any one individual in the family.

Senior Citizen Health insurance Plan: This policy is designed for the senior citizens or individuals over 60 years of age.

Surgery and Critical Illness Insurance Plan: This plan is good for those individuals, who requires treatment against critical illness, such as heart attack, kidney failure, paralysis etc. As the medical expenses of these treatments are very high, the premium of these types of policies is also high.

Maternity Health Insurance Plan: This policy covers pre and postnatal care, child delivery expenses of newborn babies.

Personal Accident Plan: This policy covers hospitalization expenses in the event of an accident. Premium will depand upon the amount of cover taken.

Benefits

1) If you are insured, you can get cashless treatments as most of the insurance companies works in collaboration with various hospital networks.
2) Insurance policy also covers pre and post hospitalization charges up to the period of 60 days, depending on your policy.
3) Insurance policy also covers the amount paid to ambulance towards the transportation of insured.
4) No Claim Bonus (NCB) is the bonus element which is paid to the insured if the insured does not file a claim for any treatment in the previous year.
5) Free health checkup is also provided by some insurers based on your previous NCBs.
6) Insurance policy also covers room expenses depending on the premium being paid by the insured.
7) You get free preventive care, like vaccines, screenings, and some check-ups, even before you meet your deductible.
8) Tax Benefit: Premium paid on Health insurance is tax deductible under section 80D of the Income Tax Act. Section 80D provides the tax deduction from the total taxable income for the payment (by any mode other than cash) of medical insruance primium paid by an individual. This tax deduction is available over and above the deduciton of Rs. 1,50,000 under Sec 80C.

Deduction that can be claimed under Section 80D at the time of filing of income tax return are:
- In case the payment of medical insurance premium is paid by the assesse for himself, spouse, dependent children - Rs. 25,000
- In case, the person insured is a Senior Citizen, the deductiom allowed should be Rs. 30,000
- In case the payment of medical insurance premium is paid by the assesss for parents, whether dependent or not - Rs. 25,000
- In case the parents of the Assessee are Senior Citizens the deduction allowed under Section 80D should be Rs. 30,000
- The deduction for senior citizens has been increased from Rs. 30,000 to Rs. 50,000 from financial year 2018-19 onwards

Healthcare Schemes by Government of India

Aam Aadmi Bima Yojana (AABY): This is a social security scheme launched in 2017 to provide healthcare facilities for rural landless individuals. The head of the household or earning member of a family will be covered under the scheme. The beneficiary should be between 18 to 59 years of age. The dependent of the beneficiary will receive Rs.30,000 upon natural death, Rs.75,000 upon accidental death or permanent disability, and Rs.37,500 on partial disability.

Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS): This scheme was launched in 1954. It provides comprehensive healthcare facilities to the central government employees, pensioners and dependents of these employees.

Employment State Insurance Scheme (ESIS): This is a social security initiative aimed at providing socio-economic protection to the working class and their dependents. The scheme ensures that all member and their family will receive full medical care from day one.

Janashree Bima Yojana (JBY): This scheme was launched in August 2000. This scheme targets people falling below the poverty line (BPL) in 45 occupational groups covered under the scheme.

Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana: Is an initiative by the government to bring access to insurance especially for the economically weaker sections of society. This scheme features affordable premiums and provides compensation to the family of the deceased.

Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana (RSBY): This scheme was launched by the Ministry of Labour and Employment. It provide health insurance coverage for families below the poverty line, the beneficiaries under this scheme can avail health cover of up to Rs.30,000. A fee of Rs. 30 is charged at the time of registration.

Universal Health Insurance Scheme (UHIS): The Indian public sector insurance companies have implemented this scheme to improve the access to healthcare for poor underprivileged families. Beneficiaries will receive reimbursement for medical expenses up to Rs.30,000 and accidental death cover up to Rs.25,000. The insurance premium for this scheme is Rs.200 per person, Rs.300 for a family of five, and Rs.400 for a family of seven
