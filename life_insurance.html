Life Insurance
(read it carefully)

Description

Life Insurance is a contract between the policy holder and the insurance company, where the life insurance company pays a specific sum to the insured individual's family upon his death. Lets put it in a more simpler way: An individual buys a life insurance policy from a insurance company, pays a amount known as the premium, if the policyholder dies while the policy is active, the insurer pays out a tax-free sum of money, known as the death benefit. The party that receive the death benefit are typically family members called beneficiaries.

How it works?

As we've already discussed, life insurance is a contract between an individual and an insurer, where the insurer guarantees payment of the death benefit to named beneficiaries upon the death of the insured, in exchange for a premium often paid regularly (depends on your policy type). The death benefit helps the beneficiaries achieve financial goals, like college, homeownership and to pay-out the debts etc.

If you die during the term, a death benefit is paid out. However, if you don't die during the term then, the premium money returned to you, without any interest and completely tax-free, as it is not considered income but simply a refund of premiums. Other events than death such as terminal illness or critical illness can also trigger payment, everything is written into your policy, so read it carefully before signing any.

Specific exclusions are often written into the contract; common examples are claims relating to suicide, fraud, war, riot, and civil commotion and some diseases like fourth stage cancers, HIV, certain types of diabetes, some rare deadly diseases and many more are not coverd under your policy, so if an insured dies due to these kind of disease, then you won't get any kind of claim. So you may wanna look out for these certain clauses.

Types of Life Insurance

Term insurance plan: These kind of plans are purchased for a fixed period of time, say 10-20 years or maybe more. These policies are cheaper as compared to other policies as there are no maturity benefit. This policy can only be claimed on the occurrence of the event.

Endowment policy: There's one difference between the term insurance plan and the endowment policy. Endowment policy comes with the extra benefit that the policyholder will receive a lump sum amount in case if he survives until the date of maturity. Rest details are same as term insurance plan.

Unit Linked Insurance Plan: These plans gives you life security and offers to build wealth in addition. Premium paid is basically devided into two parts, one for the Life insurance and another for the purpose of building wealth. This plan even offers to partially withdraw some amount.

Money Back Policy: This is same as the endowment policy, the difference is that this policy provides many survival benefits which are allotted over the period of the policy term.

Whole Life Policy: As the name says, this policy does not ends at a specific period of time rather it extends up to the whole life of the insured. This policy also provides the survival benefit to the insured. The policyholder can partially withdraw the sum insured.

Annuity/ Pension Plan: The amount collected as premium is accumulated as assets and distributed to the policyholder in form of income, annually or lump sum depending on the instruction of insured.

Consider these points before buying a Life insurance

Carefully read all Terms and conditions: After going through loads of insurance policies when you select one, read all the Terms and Conditions carefully as it contain all relevant information of an insurance policy. Make sure that you read every word of T&C and understand completely before signing an insurance policy.

Look for Lock-in period: Sometimes when you buy an insurance policy, without thinking through and later you realises that you are not completely satisfied, in such conditions some insurance companies offer a lock-in period, which is a short time usually 15 days where you can return the policy to the insurer or make another purchase.

Payment options: All insurance companies offer premium payment options as annual, semi-annual, quarterly or on monthly basis. It is good if you option for Electronic Check System (ECS) payment means it will debit your bank account with the required insurance amount when its time to pay for your premium.

Benefits

1) Insurance provides risk coverage to the insured family in form of monetary compensation.
2) There are numrous plans at your fingertips to choose from, the aplicant can choose whichever suits him/her the best.
3) These policies also cover hospitalization expenses and critical illness treatment.
4) Some insurance policies also come with the saving plan means they'll invest your money in profitable ventures.
5) Insurance policies have guaranteed sum assurd amount, which will be paid on happening of the event.
6) Insurance companies provide the option to the policyholder, that they can borrow a certain amount (option available only on selected policies).
7) Tax Benefits: Insurance premium is tax deductible under Section 80C of the income tax Act, 1961.

Deductions under Section 80C
Section 80C allows individuals and HUFs to claim tax deduction of up to Rs. 1,50,000 from their gross total income. The income tax will fund the extra amount in your bank account. Section 80C is the most important way of saving taxes and under Section 80C you can also claim tax decutions for some certain investments and payments.
