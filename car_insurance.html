Car Insurance
(save your car from unseen risks)

Description

In today’s world, a car insurance is an important policy for every car owner. Car insurance is a contract between you and the insurer, car insurance is done to protect your vehicle from unforeseen risks. It basically covers accidents, thefts. It covers against both natural and man-made calamities. In exchange for your paying a premium (often payed annually), the insurance company agrees to pay your losses as outlined in your policy.

How it works?

Car insurance works on a "use it or lose it” policy. To avail it, you have to pay a yearly premium. If you are fortunate enough to not have a serious enough accident to want to claim, you lose the premium amount you paid for protection. In case if you do not claim, you get a No Claim Bonus, which is a pretty substantial amount, starting from 20% of the premium in the first year to 50% in the sixth year, so its really great if you do not claim for minor damages.

Moreover, the primium amount of your car insurance is decided based on the Insured Declared Value (IDV). If you increase the IDV, the premium rises and if you lower it, the premium reduces and when you renew your policy after a year your IDV will decrease because of the rate of depreciation that is applied on vehicles between one and two years.

Some policies also compensate for damages to your car during natural calamities like floods or earthquakes. It also covers third-party liability where you have to pay damages to other vehicle owners. It is mandatory to buy car insurance in India irrespective of the vehicle type, i.e. personal or commercial vehicle.

Types

Comprehensive Car Insurance is one of the most valueable types of car insurace that covers both third party liabilities and damages to your own car as well.

Third-party Car Insurance is one of the most common types of car insurance; in which only damages & losses caused to a third-party person, vehicle or property are covered. Third-party liability Car Insurance is a mandate in India under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.

What affects the premium of a car insurance?

Vehicle related factors: The make, type of fuel, model etc, all matters when determining the premium of a car insurance. Usually diesel cars attract a higher premium when compared to petrol cars.
Location: The premium usually tends to be higher in urban areas and near highways.
Driver: The age and profession of the driver also plays a role in determining the premium of the car insurance.

How to renew a car insurance?

Mostly all car insurance policies last a year, so it has to be renewd after a year. If you can't remember when you bought it, don't worry. Your insurer will contact you about 21 days before renewal date to let you know when your policy will auto-renew or expire. How to claim a car insurance?

- As soon as the accident occurs, the driver or the passenger should note down the number plate of the other car in case of an accident with another car
- Contact your insurance company immediately
- File a claim with the insurance company and note down the claim reference number
- Submit the documents required by the insurance company in order to make the claim
- File an FIR at the nearest concerned police station
